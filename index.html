Halo

Halo is a popular science fiction video game franchise developed by Bungie (originally) and later continued by 343 Industries under Microsoft. The series is set in a distant future where humanity is in conflict with a coalition of alien species known as the Covenant, who are driven by religious zealotry to annihilate human civilization. Central to the story are the "Halo" rings—ancient superweapons left behind by a long-gone race called the Forerunners. These rings have the ability to wipe out all life in the galaxy, and they play a pivotal role in the ongoing struggle between humans, the Covenant, and the Flood, a parasitic alien species. The main protagonist is the super-soldier Master Chief (John-117), a Spartan, who is tasked with defending humanity. He is often accompanied by his AI companion, Cortana. The games are known for their immersive gameplay, rich universe, and complex storytelling, which touches on themes of war, sacrifice, religion, and the survival of humanity.

What this site does?

This aims to give new fans a starting point in to when, how and why they should read the books. Reason for the Books: The Halo books were created to expand on the lore, characters, and universe of the video game series in ways that a game’s limited format couldn't fully explore. They serve multiple purposes: 1.World-building: The Halo games present a vast and complex universe, but due to the constraints of gameplay, much of the intricate details, history, and political background of the universe couldn't be fully explained. The books delve into the lore of the Forerunners, the Covenant, and the origins of the Spartans, giving readers a deeper understanding of the universe. 2.Character Development: While the games primarily focus on action and missions, the novels provide more insight into the inner thoughts, emotions, and backstories of key characters like Master Chief, Cortana, and other Spartans. This adds depth to their motivations and the broader conflict. 3.Story Continuation and Expansion: The books often fill in the gaps between the games or tell stories from different perspectives. Some novels act as prequels, explaining events before the first Halo game (Halo: Combat Evolved), while others explore side stories that don't directly involve Master Chief but still impact the Halo universe. 4.Appeal to a Broader Audience: For fans who enjoy science fiction literature, the novels offer a way to engage with Halo outside of video gaming. The books also help attract readers who may not play the games but are interested in military science fiction.
